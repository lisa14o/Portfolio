About

So you’re probably wondering, ‘Who is this Lisa person, really?”. I guess the beginning is as good a spot as any to start. I was raised in Issaquah - for those unfamiliar, it’s the home of Modest Mouse, The Salmon Days parade, and a log-cabin themed McDonalds, located fifteen miles east of Seattle - oh and that Salmon Days parade, I’ve been in it not once but five times with various marching bands as a saxophonist because when you’re twelve and your name is Lisa you kind of feel like you have to pick up the saxophone.

But let’s skip ahead because we all have stuff to do today. I moved to Capitol Hill when I was nineteen and worked in a series of frozen yogurt and ice cream shops while getting my Associate’s Degree at Seattle Central College. Before you ask, I’m lactose-intolerant so, no they weren’t dream jobs and I didn’t gain a bunch of weight - I don’t know why people would always ask that last one, but they did.

Eventually I met some pretty cool kids and we started making films together. First I just assisted them here and there, then after learning the ropes I started writing, directing and producing which led to me building my own production company - OTPBJ Productions.

As much fun as it was working for minimum wage, I knew I needed to find some real, gainful employment and go back to school - this is where it gets good - so I returned to my alma mater, SCC, in the Web Development / Design program. I learned about the wonders of HTML, CSS, JavaScript, PHP and the magical world of databases. While preparing my portfolio and finishing classes I decided life wasn’t hectic enough so I started a record label with some friends and really learned the importance of teamwork and not freaking out when everything goes wrong.

I laughed, I cried, I yelled at my computer, and at the end of winter quarter 2016 I graduated from SCC and built this website. So now that we’ve gotten to know each other I hope you'll take a second to send me an email (lisa.fortino06@gmail.com) and we can take things to the next level.
